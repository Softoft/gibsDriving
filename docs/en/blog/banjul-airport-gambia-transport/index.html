Gambia Banjul Airport - Transfer, Taxi Driver

Traveling to Gambia brings its own set of unique experiences, starting right from the moment you land at the Banjul International Airport. This article aims to guide you through the airport procedures, and provide useful tips on how to secure a hassle-free airport transfer to your subsequent destination.

Touchdown in Gambia

After landing, a shuttle bus will ferry you to the main terminal building. It's worth noting that the climate inside the airport is generally warm. Therefore, storing your jackets in your carry-on luggage is advisable before disembarking. Also, keep your passport, along with all relevant documents, and a pen at hand for easy access.

Airport Development Fee

The Banjul International Airport levies a development fee of €20/$20/£20 or GMD1000 per person (excluding infants). This fee needs to be paid at the Airport Security Tax collection desk.

Immigration Checkpoint

Next, you'll be directed to the immigration control section where your passport (with visa if applicable) will be checked. You'll be asked to provide your biometrics and pose for a photograph.

Baggage Claim

Moving ahead, you'll arrive at the luggage carousel area. Be prepared for a bit of a wait here. It's advisable to carry water or other drinks in your hand luggage as the temperature may sometimes be uncomfortable. Trolleys are provided free of charge; however, if you decide to use a porter's services, be ready to tip them around £1/€1.50/$2.

Luggage Security Check

After your luggage arrives, proceed to the baggage security queue. Here, your luggage will be scanned by an X-ray machine. From this point, you may leave the main building complex. Remember to keep your luggage number handy as it may be required at the exit area.

Departing from the Airport

Once you exit the airport, you'll have multiple options for your airport transfer.

Tour Operators

If you've arrived with a tour operator, you will usually exit to the right of the main gate, where your coach will be waiting to transfer you to your resort.

Independent Travel

If you're an independent traveler, you can hire a car from a company located at the terminal or opt for one of the tourist taxis waiting outside the main gates. The cost to reach the main tourist resorts on the coast is typically around GMD1500/£18/€20/$23.

Tips for a Smooth Airport Transfer

  • If you require assistance with the arrival procedure or airport transfer, don't hesitate to reach out for help.
  • If you're not confident about the airport transfer, consider booking a chauffeur airport service in advance. This can ensure a safe and comfortable ride to your hotel.
  • If you're staying in Kololi, a Kololi transfer service can provide a convenient mode of transportation.
  • If you're not in a hurry, you might even consider a train Banjul ride or a bus Banjul ride for a more local experience.
