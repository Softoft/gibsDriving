Top 20 Must-Visit Places in Gambia

Kachikally Crocodile Pool

Experience the thrill of close encounters with crocodiles in their natural habitat at this sacred pool in Bakau.

Abuko Nature Reserve

Gambia's oldest wildlife reserve, home to a rich diversity of birds and animals, nestled within lush forests.

Bijilo Forest Park

A serene coastal park offering shaded trails, a variety of wildlife, and peaceful spots to enjoy nature.

Albert Market

Located in Banjul, this bustling market is the perfect place to shop for fabrics, crafts, and fresh produce.

Tanji Village Museum

Explore Gambian culture through exhibits on traditional dwellings, crafts, and ways of life at this open-air museum.

Sanyang Beach

One of the most beautiful beaches in Gambia, offering pristine sands, clear waters, and a relaxing ambiance.

Makasutu Culture Forest

A mystical forest rich in biodiversity and culture, offering guided tours, canoe trips, and cultural performances.

National Museum of Gambia

Delve into the history, culture, and heritage of Gambia with a visit to this informative museum in Banjul.

Kunta Kinteh Island

Formerly James Island, this UNESCO World Heritage site offers a poignant look into the history of the transatlantic slave trade.

Fort Bullen

This historic fort offers stunning views and a unique insight into Gambia's colonial history.

Stone Circles of Senegambia

Discover the mysterious ancient stone circles spread across Gambia and Senegal, believed to be burial sites.

Gambia River National Park

Experience the beauty and diversity of the Gambia River with a boat tour, spotting wildlife and scenic landscapes.

Pipeline Mosque

A significant religious site in The Gambia, known for its beautiful architecture and spiritual atmosphere.

Wassu Stone Circles

Visit the Wassu Stone Circles, a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its archaeological significance.

Banjul State House

The official residence of the President of Gambia, notable for its architectural beauty and historical importance.

Arch 22

A striking monument and museum offering panoramic views of Banjul from its top, commemorating a military coup.

Juffureh Village

Known for its connection to Alex Haley's "Roots", this village offers a deep dive into Gambian history and culture.

Kotu Beach

A popular spot for tourists, known for its sandy beaches, local markets, and vibrant nightlife.

Gunjur Village

Experience the authentic Gambian village life and explore the local culture, traditions, and beautiful beaches.

Serekunda Market

The largest market in Gambia, offering everything from textiles to electronics, and a true taste of local life.
